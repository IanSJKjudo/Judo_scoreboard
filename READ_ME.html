Score-Judo

A portable, offline judo scoreboard


  1. Copy this entire folder into your local device.
  2. Connect your projection device (e.g. monitor).
  3. Read the help text below, and the help text directly below the scoreboard itself.
  4. Press the start button at the bottom of this page to launch the program.

Most laptops will automatically detect that a second monitor is now connected. If not, force it to 'detect'. Set the second monitor (the TV) up on laptop to 'duplicate' your desktop (not 'extend'). On a Windows this can be done through the Settings application, or the WIN+P shortcut.

Spend a few minutes reading the help text located on the Score-Judo display. There may be several features not well known about but which will be of use - especially at a tournament running more than one mat, including alternate sound effects for each mat, welcome messages at top of the scoreboard, mat numbering etc.

Practice a little and get used to its behaviour for winding back scoring mistakes, Osaekomi / Toketa situations and such like. Scorejudo defaults to the MONSTER and Special Needs format where more than one Ippon is often allowed - therefore when the ref announces IPPON for a seniors match the scorer still needs to actively invoke ippon and stop the clock.

To switch the colour scheme from traditional red-white to the IJF white-blue, edit ijf_or_traditional.js within the scorejudo_files folder.
